## Underlying public-domain sources

The ACR draws on texts that are in the public domain (Hebrew Bible,
Dead Sea Scrolls, Ethiopian Orit Ge'ez canon, 1 Enoch, Jubilees,
etc.). **The underlying public-domain texts themselves are not
claimed as proprietary.** What IS proprietary is:

- The *particular compilation* — which texts to include, in what
  order, under what organizing structure.
- The *translation choices* made for each passage.
- The *editorial selections* — which variants to follow, which
  footnotes to include, which passages to emphasize.
- All *original study materials, summaries, commentary, questions,
  hints, and framing*.

In copyright law this is the standard "compilation" protection: a
compilation of public-domain works, with original editorial and
creative decisions, is itself protected.
